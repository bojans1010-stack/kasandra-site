    Enter at or inside the zone. Price usually dips into the entry area. Get in there — or split into up to 3 small entries across the zone to average a better price. If price already ran far past the entry, let it go.
  3. 3
    Attach SL and TPs immediately. The moment you enter, set the stop and the take-profits on the order. Never "watch it manually." The stop is the strategy, not a suggestion.
  4. 4
    Bank TP1, then go risk-free. When price hits TP1, close a third and move your stop to break-even (entry). From here the trade cannot lose — worst case is zero.
  5. 5
    Trail the rest to TP2, TP3, and the runner. Take another third at TP2, another at TP3. Let the final piece (TP4) ride for the big moves with the stop trailing behind.
  6. 6
    Missed it? Skip it. If you weren't there in time, do not chase a signal that already left. Forcing a bad entry costs more than missing a good one. The next signal is always coming.
  7. 7
    When the system is quiet, stay quiet. "No setups / filtered" means the market isn't offering a good trade right now. That silence protects you — don't invent trades to fill the boredom.

The principles
🎯

The 1% rule

Risk a small, fixed slice of your account on each signal — 1% is the professional standard. At 1%, you'd need 100 losing trades in a row to blow up. That never happens with an 80% win-rate system. This single habit is why pros survive decades.

🔁

Losing streaks are normal

Even at 80% win rate, streaks of 3–5 losses happen regularly, and 8 will happen eventually. Size so 8 straight stops still leaves you trading. The traders who blow up aren't wrong more often — they're sized too big when the inevitable streak arrives.

⚖️

Max 3 positions · no overlap

Kasandra never holds more than 3 trades at once, and skips signals within 15 points of an open one. This caps your worst-case exposure. When sizing, remember: 3 open trades = 3× your single-signal risk if they all stop together.

🍌

Move stop to break-even

When a trade hits TP1, move the stop to entry. From that moment the trade can't lose — worst case is zero. This is built into every signal and it's why our partial-win trades rarely turn into full losses.

📰

Respect spread & news

Wide spread and high-impact news (CPI, NFP, FOMC) can spike price through a stop. The system already filters compressed, low-momentum tape — and you should size down or sit out around red-flag news. Surviving the spike matters more than catching it.

📈

Scale with equity, not hope

Start at the size your capital justifies. When the account grows, your fixed-% lot grows with it automatically — that's compounding. Never jump to bigger lots after a win out of excitement, or after a loss to "win it back." Let the math size you.

⭐ Kasandra's Golden Rules
  1. 1Never risk more than 1–2% per signal. If a single loss hurts emotionally, you're too big.
  2. 2Always use the stop. Every signal has one. No "I'll watch it manually." The stop is the strategy.
  3. 3Take partials and move to break-even. Bank TP1, protect the rest. Greed gives profits back.
  4. 4Max 3 trades open. More positions ≠ more profit, just more correlated risk.
  5. 5Survive the bad week. Your only job in a drawdown is to still be here for the recovery.
  6. 6Let the account grow you. Scale size with equity, never with emotion.
